Experience

Prince of Persia the Shadow and the Flame was my first experience of handling the level design process from preproduction to production phase. Being the primary level designer, my responsibility during the preproduction phase was to coordinate with the programmer, level artist and the technical artist to define the pipeline and workflow for efficient level creation during the production phase. Along with the technical artist, I came up with an innovative way of blocking out the level in 3D Studio Max and Photoshop. It allowed for quick iteration of layout and made the whole level designing process very efficient. I also came up with the specifications for an editor tool required for gameplay integration, which the technical artist developed for 3D Studio Max. This allowed the design team to work completely within 3D Studio Max. It also allowed the design team and the art team to work independently yet stay synchronized with each other through the same toolset.

 

Once we moved into production, I was tasked with designing 5 levels, 3 cave levels and the first two temple levels. I also designed two boss fights, one for cave and one for the ruin level. I gained a lot of experience in designing combat scenarios, set-pieces, adding narrative to gameplay through enemy staging and level art composition.
